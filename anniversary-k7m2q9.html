The Card Ritual

Cards get exchanged Thu morning at home, before the day starts.

  1. Slow morning. Coffee. Kids out of the picture (grandparents / camp / sitter — sort logistics ahead).
  2. Sit down together — porch, breakfast nook, wherever's quiet. Phones away.
  3. Exchange cards. Read to yourselves first, then trade thoughts on what you wrote.
  4. Don't rush. This is the emotional center of the trip — everything else is celebration around it.

Alternate slot: at Kai Zan between courses if you'd rather share cards over dinner. Sushi bars are built for that kind of pacing.

Add-on ritual to consider: "20 memories" list — take turns naming one memory from each of the 20 years, one at a time, over dessert Thu night. Slow, no phones, one memory per year.

Backup options (if anything falls through)

Restaurants

  • If Kai Zan is full Thu: Thattu (Kerala Indian tasting menu, Avondale) — same anniversary vibe, different cuisine. Or Passerotto (Korean-Italian, Andersonville).
  • If Akahoshi Thu lunch is booked: try Fri lunch on the way back through the city — swap Sugar Fixe for Akahoshi. Or Sticky Rice (Lincoln Sq, Northern Thai) for lunch instead.
  • If Boonie's Wed is booked: Kettlestrings Tavern in Oak Park (walkable, cozy) or Marion Street Cheese Market.

Comedy

  • If Second City is sold out: iO Theater (Wrigleyville, longform improv) or Chicago Magic Lounge — but that requires rearranging dinner earlier / lighter.
  • If you'd rather music: Green Mill jazz (Uptown, walk-in usually) — legendary room, would work as a late-night stop after Kai Zan instead of comedy.

Weather / mood pivots

  • Rain Thu afternoon: Art Institute or MCA instead of Riverwalk.
  • Too much day, need downtime: skip the afternoon activity, come home to Oak Park after Akahoshi and rest, drive back in for Kai Zan + comedy.
  • Kids come home early Thu: collapse the comedy — dessert after Kai Zan (Margie's Candies is close) and home.
