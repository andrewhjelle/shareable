The Card Ritual

Cards get exchanged Thu morning at home, before the day starts.

  1. Slow morning. Coffee. Kids out of the picture (grandparents / camp / sitter — sort logistics ahead).
  2. Sit down together — porch, breakfast nook, wherever's quiet. Phones away.
  3. Exchange cards. Read to yourselves first, then trade thoughts on what you wrote.
  4. Don't rush. This is the emotional center of the trip — everything else is celebration around it.

Alternate slot: during the lakefront walk Thu afternoon. Find a bench with a skyline view, open them there. Public but private.

Add-on ritual to consider: "20 memories" list — take turns naming one memory from each of the 20 years, one at a time. Slow, no phones. Great during the lake walk or over Kai Zan's slower courses.

Lakefront Trail
Museum Campus → Navy Pier · ~2.5 miles · leisurely

Suggested route: Start at Museum Campus (Adler / Shedd / Field). Walk north along the Lakefront Trail past Grant Park, Buckingham Fountain, Millennium Park, then all the way to Navy Pier. Skyline on your left, lake on your right. Roughly 2-3 hours if you're strolling and stopping.

Best stops:

  • Milton Lee Olive Park — quiet spot with the best skyline view. Great card-opening bench.
  • Millennium Park — Bean photo, Crown Fountain, Lurie Garden
  • Chicago Riverwalk — detour west if you want water on both sides
  • Navy Pier — turnaround point. Ferris wheel if you're feeling it.
🚶 2-3 hrs ☀ Bring water + sunscreen 👟 Comfortable shoes

Kai Zan
Twin brothers · West Town · ~30 seats · sushi-nerd's favorite

Twin brothers Melvin & Carlo Vizconde run a tiny, warm sushi room. Not omakase-only — they have à la carte too. Ask about off-menu specials of the night. Tell them it's your 20th when you book; they may adjust the pacing so the meal builds toward something.

This is the main event. Unhurried 2-hour dinner. Order the omakase if it's available, or work through the nigiri list slowly. Between courses is a natural moment for the "20 memories" ritual.

Backup options (if anything falls through)

Restaurants

  • If Kai Zan is full Thu: Thattu (Kerala Indian tasting menu, Avondale) — different cuisine, same anniversary energy. Or Passerotto (Korean-Italian, Andersonville).
  • If Akahoshi Wed is booked: Boonie's (Filipino, Pilsen) slides right back in as Wed dinner.
  • If Sugar Fixe Fri is closed: Buzz Cafe or Kettlestrings, both walkable.

Comedy

  • If Laugh Factory's lineup is meh: Second City Mainstage ("Pandemonium, Please Hold" 114th Revue) — sketch + improv. Note the timing: their Thu show requires reshuffling Kai Zan.
  • If you'd rather improv: iO Theater (Wrigleyville, longform Harold).
  • If you'd rather music: Green Mill jazz (Uptown, walk-in usually) — walk in after Kai Zan for a late-night jazz cap instead of comedy.
  • If you'd rather magic: Chicago Magic Lounge — 7 PM Signature Show. Would require dinner rearranged (early / à la carte / lighter).

Weather / mood pivots

  • Rain Thu afternoon: Skip the lakefront walk. Go to the Art Institute or MCA instead.
  • Too much day, need downtime: Shorten the walk. Sit at a park bench with coffee for an hour. This is a rest day disguised as an activity.
  • Kids come home early Thu: Skip Kai Zan post-show, do dessert instead (Margie's Candies is close to Second City) and come home.
