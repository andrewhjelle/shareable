A Naperville plan · from Andrew

Six weeks, six activities, and one weekly movement class.

A picked list of things to try, one at a time, with real details checked.

Six weeks. One new activity to try each week. Not six commitments, just six single trips. Whatever sticks after six weeks becomes something regular. Whatever doesn't, drop and forget.

Each pick has been checked. Runs currently, not defunct. Age range noted. Sign-up or walk-in noted. The point is not filling out a schedule. The point is finding two or three activities worth going back to.

Zepbound context: the medication is doing the weight side well. The one thing worth adding on top is gentle movement, so muscle is preserved while weight comes off. The very first section below is built around that, sized to accommodate the knee replacements.

The Weekly Movement Layer

Runs parallel to the six-week rotation

A gentle strength or mobility class, knee-friendly

Zepbound removes weight but can also remove muscle if activity stays low. One weekly class is enough to offset that at a very inactive baseline. Four modalities work for someone with a double knee replacement: chair yoga, SilverSneakers Classic, a gentle water class, and tai chi. All are seated or low-impact. No squats, no running, no jumping.

Week 6

Week 6 · One conversation at the church office

Midweek church small groups and Stephen Ministry

Most local churches run their deepest friendships through midweek small groups, not Sunday service. Two under-the-radar openings are worth asking about at any church office: a Stephen Ministry program, and the list of active women's small groups or Bible studies currently taking new members.

Stephen Ministers are trained lay caregivers who make regular visits to congregants going through hard seasons. It is essentially hospital or elder ministry inside a congregation, which lines up with the same background used for Week 5. There are two roles: receiving a Stephen Minister, or training to be one (about a 50-hour commitment upfront).

Naperville's living-history site. Docents give tours. Applications are open year-round but training begins winter (Jan/Feb 2027 cohort). Commitment: twice a month for five months. Contact: 630-420-6104 · nsvolunteer@naperville.il.us

Wheaton, ~20 minutes from Naperville. The closer version of prison ministry. Sheriff's office main line 630-407-2400, ask for the chaplaincy coordinator.

Illinois runs a Certified Recovery Support Specialist path through IAODAPCA. Volunteer hours count toward it. Relevant if peer counseling ever pulls at more than volunteer hours would. iaodapca.org

After Six Weeks

The Keep-Drop Rule

At the end of six weeks, keep the two activities that made the week feel worth looking forward to. Drop the rest, guilt-free.

Two weekly activities is a good place to land: enough to fill out the week, not so much that it becomes another job. Once those two are established, a third can be added later or left alone.

Also fine: none stuck. Not every activity clicks on the first visit. If that happens, Nichols Library's reference desk is a good reset point; they know what is new in town this season.

The Dating-App Path

The plan above is the main event. If an app profile stays open alongside, three options are more reliable than Facebook Dating.

  • OurTime (ourtime.com). Largest dating site for 50+. Real verification, most users looking for actual relationships.
  • SilverSingles (silversingles.com). 50+, questionnaire-based matching. More curated than swipe apps.
  • Match (match.com). Strong 50+ population, better fraud detection than most.

All three charge for messaging, which is actually a plus: the fee filters out scammers running dozens of profiles at once.

Two Rules, No Exceptions

Never click a link he sends. Not a photo album, not a video, not a shipping tracker. Photos should just appear in the message. If they arrive as a link, that is not how normal people share them.

Never send money for any reason. Not a loan, not gift cards, not "temporary" help with a bank problem, not travel money. This rule holds no matter how long the conversation has been going. Real partners understand it and do not push.

The face-swap check. Before an in-person meeting, do a live video call. At some point during the call, ask him to turn his head to the side and to wave a hand near his face. Face-swap software breaks visibly during side profiles and hand movement. A real person does either without noticing.

The whole plan is optional. Everything on it is a suggestion, not a task. If Week 1 does not happen because a week is bad, that is fine. The next week works. If something on the list does not sound right, cross it off and swap in something else. The point is a rhythm, not a finished list.
