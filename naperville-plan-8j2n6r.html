A Naperville plan · from Andrew

One thing a week, six weeks in.

A picked list of local activities, no pressure on any

Six weeks. One new thing to try each week. Not six commitments, just six single trips. Whatever sticks after six weeks becomes something regular. Whatever doesn't, drop and forget.

Each pick is checked. Runs now, not defunct. Age group noted. Sign-up or walk-in noted. So the first trip doesn't turn into an empty parking lot or a room full of thirty-year-olds. The point is not filling out a schedule. The point is finding two or three that make you look forward to the week.

Zepbound note: the meds are working, and the early indigestion has settled. The one thing to layer on top is gentle movement, so you keep muscle while you lose weight. The very next section is built around that, sized to your knees.

Zepbound is doing the weight side well. The one thing worth layering on top is gentle movement, so you keep muscle while you lose weight. Nothing scary. No lifting, no squats, no running, no impact on the knees. One class a week makes a real difference at your baseline.

Chair yoga · SilverSneakers Classic · Gentle water class · Tai Chi. All four are seated or low-impact, taught for people rebuilding from replacements or years off the map.

Week 6Church, midweek, in what you already know how to do

Week 6 · one visit to the church office
Two questions at your church that unlock everything else
Familiar ground Free or workbook fee Weekday, not Sunday
Church is already your community. The deep friendships form in the midweek smaller groups, not in the pew on Sunday. There are two specific questions you can ask the office that will surface everything worth doing.
Question 1 · Stephen Ministry

Ask if your church has a Stephen Ministry or any lay pastoral care team. Stephen Ministers are trained lay caregivers who visit congregants going through hard seasons. It is hospital / elder ministry inside your own church, and it lines up exactly with your background.

After 6 weeksHow to decide what to keep

The keep-drop rule

At the end of six weeks, keep the two that made you look forward to going. Drop the rest, guilt-free.

Two weekly activities is a good place to land. Enough to fill out the week, not so much that it becomes another job. Once those two are established, you can decide whether to add a third or leave it.

Also fine: none stuck. Not everything clicks the first time. If that happens, go back to Nichols Library and ask the desk what is new in town this season. They always know.

The dating-app pathIf you want to try this alongside

The plan above is the main event. If you also want an app profile open in the background, three that are better than Facebook Dating.

  • OurTime (ourtime.com). Largest dating site for 50+. Real verification, most users looking for actual relationships.
  • SilverSingles (silversingles.com). 50+, questionnaire-based matching. More curated than swipe apps.
  • Match (match.com). Strong 50+ population, better fraud detection than most.

All three charge for messaging. That is actually a plus. The fee filters out scammers running dozens of profiles at once.

Two rules, no exceptions

Never click a link he sends. Not a photo album, not a video, not a shipping tracker. Photos should just appear in the message. If they arrive as a link, that is not how normal people share them.

Never send money for any reason. Not a loan, not gift cards, not "temporary" help with a bank problem, not travel money. This rule holds no matter how long you've been talking. Real partners understand it and don't push.

The face-swap check: before meeting anyone from an app, do a live video call. Ask them at some point to turn their head to the side and wave a hand near their face. Face-swap software breaks visibly during side profiles and hand movement. A real person does either without noticing.

NoteThe frame

The whole plan is optional. Everything on it is a suggestion, not a task. If Week 1 doesn't happen because a week is bad, that is fine. Try it again the next week. If something doesn't sound like you, cross it off and swap in something else. The point is having a rhythm, not finishing a list.
