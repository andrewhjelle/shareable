§ 01 The problem Why a daily needs a rotating art direction

Most daily newsletters die at week four.

The original Beauty Co Daily is editorially strong: ordered sections, a clear hero, real signal-to-noise. But editorially strong is not the same as visually durable. By the fourth Tuesday in a row, the masthead becomes wallpaper. The hero grid becomes furniture. The reader's eye learns the page and stops looking.

The fix isn't "more variety inside each issue." That dilutes hierarchy. The fix is treating the daily like a real magazine — where the inside is structured and predictable, but the cover and art direction change with the week, the news, and the mood of what's being published.

A daily that re-skins itself is a daily that gets opened a fifth, sixth, twelfth time — out of curiosity, not duty.

The architecture below separates what readers need to recognize (sections, content, hierarchy) from what they should find newly delightful (palette, type, geometry, texture, motion). Recognition keeps trust. Delight keeps attention.

Quality is the constraint, not the variable. Every composition the skill produces passes through a design critic that gates on legibility, hierarchy, art-historical legibility, and mood-to-news fit before publish.

§ 02 Architecture Three layers, separated cleanly — plus a conditional content kit

Recognition stays. Direction changes.

The architecture layer is invariant. The module layer composes. The atelier layer rotates. A fourth, conditional content kit (tables, charts, sparklines, quotes, timelines) is pulled in only when the day's content earns it — never to fill space.
Layer 01 · Invariant

Architecture

The reader's mental map

Seven sections, always in this order: Hero, Since-Last, Trends, Numbers, On Our Plate, Vitals, Archive, Resources. The TOC, the section numbering, and the narrative arc never change. Trust is built here.

A reader who has read three issues already knows where to look for numbers. We never make them re-learn.

Fixed order Fixed labels Fixed TOC Fixed hierarchy
Layer 02 · Composed daily

Modules

Picked from a library; assembled to fit the day

Each section has 3–4 layout variants. Hero can be a Billboard, a split Aperture, a Filmstrip, or a Fullbleed Quote — the skill picks based on the day's content shape: one big story vs. many small, charts vs. quotes, jubilant vs. somber.

Composition is rule-driven, not random. The library is the menu, the content is the order.

Hero × 4 Trend × 4 Numbers × 4 Plate × 3 Archive × 3 ≈ 22 variants
Layer 03 · Rotated

Atelier

The art direction — palette, type, geometry, texture, motion

One baseline (Sage Paper) plus six guest directors, each rooted in a real art movement. The baseline runs most weeks. Guests are events.

Within an atelier, the seven sliders allow micro-variation day-to-day.

Layer 04 · Conditional

Content kit

Tables, charts, quotes, timelines — pulled in only when earned

Not every issue needs a chart. The skill only includes a module when the content triggers it (≥3 metrics with trends → sparklines; ≥4 dated events → timeline; a sourced 12-word line → pull-quote).

Each module ships seven mood-aware renders. Same data shape, different visual.

5c Content module kit Optional inclusions — pulled in only when the data triggers them

The skill never invents a chart to fill space.

The biggest tell of generated design is decoration without purpose — a sparkline for vibes, a quote that no one said, a table built from prose. The content module kit is the rule that keeps that from happening here.

Each module has a trigger condition. If the day's content packet satisfies the trigger, the module is offered. The composer can still decline if hierarchy gets crowded, but it cannot include a module the data didn't earn.

Each module ships seven mood renders. Same data shape, same trigger, different visual.

§ 5.5 Deep-dive shell The standalone page that keeps the issue's look but opens room to read

Same atelier. Room to read.

A deep dive is a separate HTML page linked from the daily, not a section inside it. It must still feel like the same publication. The shell does that with two moves: it reuses the issue's atelier motion as a fixed, full-page centerpiece behind everything, then floats a translucent reading column over it. The look carries from the main page; the column gives long-form prose and diagrams room to breathe, with the atelier showing through and in the margins on either side.
Three structural layers (class names as shipped in the Iss. 008 standalones):

The shell, layer by layer

1 · Page centerpiece (.page-cp): the atelier's motion artwork — the same cp-chrome SVG as the parent issue — pinned position:fixed; inset:0; z-index:-1 at ~0.85 opacity. It sits behind all content and never scrolls. This is what makes the standalone read as the same issue. Inherit it via body data-atelier="…" set to the parent issue's atelier; never restyle the motion per deep dive.

2 · Masthead (.masthead): a position:sticky top bar with backdrop-filter: blur(14px) frosted glass, a back-link to the issue, and a mono meta line. Keeps wayfinding visible over the moving background.

3 · Reading frame (.dd-frame): a centered column, max-width:1080px; margin:0 auto, filled with translucent color-mix(in srgb, var(--paper-card) 86%, transparent) + backdrop-filter: blur(4px) and thin left/right rules. The translucency is the whole point: the atelier motion reads softly through the column and fully in the margins beside it, so the page has the issue's texture while the text stays legible. This is the "room for content" — a calm reading surface laid over the live look.

What goes inside the frame

The reading column carries the deep-dive content components: .dd-hero (kicker + serif headline + deck + byline), .dd-keystats (a 4-up bordered stat bar under the hero), numbered .dd-section blocks for the body, the §06 diagram patterns, the mandatory .ptest pressure-test block, then .dd-sources and .dd-foot. The atelier styles type, accent, and motion; the shell and these components stay fixed across ateliers so every deep dive reads as one series.

The rule: a deep dive = the parent issue's atelier motion (fixed, full-page) + a centered translucent reading frame. Keep the look; open the room. If a future deep dive needs a literally right-weighted or asymmetric column instead of the centered frame, that's a deliberate variant — document it here rather than restyling ad hoc.

§ 06 Diagrams The visual repertoire · charts, mockups, comparison tiles, pullquotes

Every deep dive earns at least one diagram.

The deep-dive standalones introduced eight reusable visual patterns. Each pattern has a fixed structural role (compare, contrast, illustrate, quote) and a consistent visual treatment (rounded gradient containers, mono-uppercase captions, two-tone fills) so they read as one family across ateliers. The atelier supplies palette + accent; the pattern supplies the geometry.

Five rules that apply to every diagram

  1. One pattern per role. Don't pair two growth-bar charts on the same page; pair growth-bars with hero-grid (different roles).
  2. Captions before fills. Every diagram opens with a FIG. N · TITLE caption in Geist Mono uppercase. The figure number is the reader's anchor.
  3. Two-tone gradients. Wins go green→deeper green, losses go red→deeper red, neutrals go gray→deeper gray. Never use the same color for win and loss; never use cobalt for win.
  4. Rounded gradient containers. Container border-radius: 18px, soft shadow, gradient background tinted with the atelier's two primary colors at low opacity.
  5. Caption then chart, never label-only. A bar without a caption is a sketch, not a diagram.

§ 07 Animation Universal defaults, per-mood signatures, one centerpiece per issue

Alive, but restrained.

Three rules in order: (1) universal defaults apply to every issue regardless of atelier. (2) Each atelier adds one motion signature plus up to two supporting in-content motions (§13i) — one to three total, layered at different speeds. (3) One centerpiece per issue — a single full-bleed animated anchor, reserved for covers and standalone deep-dive heroes, never over body text. Glossary used identically here and in §13: a signature is the atelier's recognizable gesture; a supporting motion is in-content (§13i); the centerpiece is the one performing anchor.

Universal defaults

Section entry: Intersection-observer fade-in, 240ms ease-out, 12px y-offset. Once per section.

Hover states: color shift only. No scale, no bounce, no shadow swell.

Live indicators: blinking dot on the LIVE badge; 1.6s ease-in-out.

Scoped by context, not a blanket ban. Never: scroll-tied parallax, springy/bouncy easing, autoplay video, cursor-following, and fast or high-contrast animated gradients or dense particle fields over body text. Covers / heroes only: one §13b background system. Reading pages: the slow (15-35s), low-contrast, edge-weighted ambient gradients and sparse drifting motes of §13j, plus the in-content motion of §13i, are the sanctioned exception.

The centerpiece rule
Each issue gets one animated anchor element — a kinetic headline, a single chart that draws in, a column of light that breathes. Never more than one centerpiece per issue (this cap is on the centerpiece — not on the calm §13j background or the §13i in-content motion, which serve reading). Motion that's everywhere is motion that disappears.

Per-mood signatures

§ 10 The critic Nine gates between draft and publish

Variety, but with standards.

The biggest risk in a rotating system is drift into slop. The critic is a small, opinionated rubric the skill self-runs after composition. Failing a gate triggers regeneration of the offending module — not the whole issue. Two retries before fallback.
01
Contrast hits AA on every text/background pair
Auto-test
No exceptions for "design effect." Dark on dark fails the gate; the skill nudges the palette one step toward higher contrast and re-runs.
02
Accent color appears at least three times
Auto-count
A single red dot does not an accent make. If the atelier's primary accent shows up fewer than three times, the design lacks visual rhythm; rebalance.
03
Type-family count ≤ 3
Auto-count
Display + body + mono is the ceiling. Adding a fourth (e.g. a script) is allowed only if it's used in exactly one place. Anything more reads as a font sampler.
04
At least one section breaks the dominant grid
Auto-detect
If every section uses the same column structure, the issue feels mechanical. The composer must introduce one deliberate asymmetry — a full-bleed quote, a single oversize number, a mosaic.
05
Hero element has clear visual gravity
Heuristic
The eye should land first on the hero, second on the bullets. Computed: hero display text occupies ≥4× the area of any single body paragraph in the first 600px.
06
Mood matches the news tone
LLM check
If the day's content is dominated by layoffs / failures / regulation crackdowns, the system rejects Terrazzo (playful) and Reactor (ardent). Allowed: Aperture, Ukiyo, Dunes. Mood is mandatory, not suggested.
07
Art-historical lineage is legible
LLM check
A second model reads the rendered issue and is asked: "name the art movement this issue draws from." If it can't pick within the atelier's declared lineage tag, the styling is too generic — re-render with stronger grammar.
08
No AI slop patterns present
Heuristic + LLM
Explicit pattern matches: smooth radial color-gradients without purpose; generic stock-photo subjects (people centered with soft focus, "warm office lighting"); decorative-but-meaningless illustration; over-rendered SVG figures; symmetrical "AI bowl of fruit" compositions; any AI-generated face or hands on a human. Any one triggers a regenerate.
09
Every content module was earned
Auto-check
Each pulled-in module (table, chart, sparkline, quote, timeline, etc.) is checked against its trigger condition. If the trigger isn't satisfied by the day's content packet, the module was invented to fill space — strip it.

Why a critic, and not a checklist for the editor?

Because the editor is shipping a daily. A daily has no time for a 7-item visual audit, and the moment audit becomes optional, slop creeps in.

The critic runs in seconds and only escalates the items it can't fix itself. The editor sees, at most, a single "needs your eyes" flag — and only on the rare day the system can't self-correct.

Example · 22 May
"Composer selected Terrazzo for Iss. 004. Critic Gate 06 flagged tone mismatch — the EL–Puig termination + Anthropic Q2 mix reads as tectonic, not playful. Selector retried, picked Reactor; Gate 08 confirmed no slop patterns; Gate 09 confirmed all three content modules (sparkline-row, timeline, source-list) were trigger-earned. Shipped in 4.2s."
§ 11 Skill spec What the daily-builder skill reads on its way to compose

The instruction sheet, for the LLM.

A condensed, machine-readable version of everything above. The composing skill reads this verbatim; the critic skill reads the nine gates from §09.

1. Inputs

  • content_pack.json — the day's curated stories, stats, project updates, archive deltas.
  • atelier_rotation.json — the week's atelier order plus any human override.
  • reference_pool/*.png + tokens.json — current week's reference images and their tokenized palette/mood data.
  • telemetry_30d.json — last 30 days of opens / scroll depth / dwell by atelier & content profile.
  • previous_issue.html — yesterday's issue, for recency & variation guards.

2. Classify (§07a)

  1. Tag the content profile. 1–3 tags from: sober · tectonic · synthesis · milestone · reflective · playful · crisis. Conservative bias: prefer sober if uncertain. crisis is a hard tag and blocks Terrazzo + Mural.

3. Select (§07b)

  1. Filter ateliers by profile-defaults ∩ mood_allowed ∩ (last-48h-not-used, except Sage).
  2. Weight remaining candidates by engagement score for this profile × a Sage 2× floor × a pool-cap (no atelier > 25% of last 30 issues).
  3. Pick via weighted-random.
  4. Nudge one slider one position max (discrete). If yesterday was the same atelier, the nudge is mandatory.

4. Compose

  1. Pick a layout from the atelier's layouts_allowed list.
  2. Pick a component variant per section by content shape:
    section: hero if single_dominant_story → Billboard elif structural_pair → Aperture elif quotable_line → FullbleedQuote elif multi_story_arc → Filmstrip section: trends if 3–5_equal_weight → StackOfTrends elif one_dominant → ColumnBroken elif weekend_roundup → Mosaic elif single_essay_day → SingleTrendEssay section: numbers if one_giant_figure → OversizeOne elif markets_heavy → Ticker elif standard → StatGrid
  3. Offer earned content modules (§5c). For each module, evaluate its trigger against the content pack; include only if it triggers.
    data_table: cols≥3 AND rows≥3 AND comparable bar_chart: buckets in [3,12] line_chart: dated_series AND points≥6 spark_row: metrics≥3 AND each_has_trend timeline: dated_events≥4 AND single_arc pull_quote: sourced AND words≥12 AND named_human compare_grid: entities≥2 AND axes≥3 callout: editor_flag_present source_list: cited_sources≥3
  4. Pick centerpiece (§06): one anchor element — kinetic-headline | drawn-chart | lit-timeline | animated-stat. Never two.
  5. Apply atelier tokens via CSS custom properties on the document root. Components read tokens only — never hard-code color or font.
  6. Anti-repeat guard. No component variant repeats for ≥3 issues in a row. No content module repeats for ≥2 issues in a row (unless triggered identically).

5. Atelier token blocks

{ "sage": { "palette": { "bg":"#F1F2EA", "ink":"#0F1417", "a1":"#E84478", "a2":"#0B2545", "a3":"#F5C03D", "a4":"#6E8B5C" }, "fonts": { "display":"Instrument Serif", "body":"Geist", "mono":"Geist Mono" }, "geometry": { "radius":0, "rule":1, "grid":"canonical" }, "texture": "clean", "motion": { "ease":"cubic-bezier(.16,1,.3,1)", "ms":240, "signature":"none" }, "mood_allowed": ["sober","tectonic","synthesis","crisis"], "layouts_allowed": ["canonical","asymmetric"], "lineage": "Editorial restraint · The Browser / Pentagram", "baseline": true }, "reactor": { // riso · mood_allowed: ["tectonic","milestone","playful"] }, "aperture": { // De Stijl · mood_allowed: ["sober","tectonic","synthesis"] }, "ukiyo": { // Edo woodblock · mood_allowed: ["synthesis","reflective"] }, "mural": { // Romantic · mood_allowed: ["reflective","synthesis"]; blocked on crisis }, "terrazzo": { // Memphis · mood_allowed: ["playful"]; blocked on crisis }, "dunes": { // cinematic · mood_allowed: ["milestone","tectonic","reflective"] } }

6. Critique (§09)

Run nine gates: contrast AA · accent ≥3× · type families ≤3 · grid break · hero gravity · mood↔news fit · lineage legible · anti-slop pattern absent · every content module earned. Gate failure → regenerate only the failing module. Two retries max; on third, fall back to Sage Paper and write review_required.json.

7. Outputs

  • issue.html — the day's published page.
  • issue.meta.json — atelier, content profile, layout, modules used, references that shaped the palette, gate results, centerpiece, nudges applied.
  • telemetry_seed.json — keys to log: open rate, scroll depth per section, dwell per atelier, re-roll flag (joined upstream).

8. Cadence & ownership

  • Daily, automated. Classify → Select → Compose → Critique → Publish. ~10–25 seconds.
  • Weekly, human-curated. Refresh reference_pool/ from museum APIs + Are.na + editorial sources; review previous week's telemetry; set next week's atelier order.
  • Monthly, editor-led. Retire under-performing ateliers; introduce at most one new lineage. Cap rotation pool at 8 (1 baseline + 7 guests) to keep each individuality recognizable.

§ 13 The compendium A mix-and-match toolbelt for building an atelier from any artist

Pick the parts. Compose the look.

Every atelier is one recipe drawn from these shelves: a background system, one to three individual motions, a transition, a type set, a texture, an emphasis device, and a palette strategy. The atelier-from-artist skill researches the artist, then selects from these. Example, validated for Arcimboldo: ferrofluid + a trailing paintstroke + the combine mechanic, on a chiaroscuro ground. Recipes are at the bottom. The governing rule: motion serves reading. On a page meant to be read, the background sets a calm mood at the edges (§13j) and in-content motion (§13i) draws the eye and rewards interaction — nothing performs over the text. Reserve the louder hero systems (§13b) and dramatic transitions for covers and standalone deep-dive heroes, not the body.

13a · Individual motions attach to any element

Single-purpose animations. Layer two or three at different speeds for richness; never run one frantic loop. All respect prefers-reduced-motion.

Ferrofluid / metaballs
SVG goo filter
Blobs that merge and split organically. The "combining" reads as parts fusing into a whole — Arcimboldo, fluid M&A, organic moods.

staggered transform

layered CSS rotate

radial dot pattern

staggered opacity
Dots that twinkle in and out, forming shapes from points. Seurat, Signac, divisionist palettes.
